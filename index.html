While croissants are often associated with France, it is believed that their origins can be traced back to Austria. Legend has it that the croissant was invented in Vienna in 1683, when the city was under siege by the Ottoman Turks. As the story goes, Viennese bakers who were working in the early morning hours heard the sound of the Turks tunneling beneath the city and sounded the alarm. The bakers then created a crescent-shaped pastry in the shape of the Ottoman flag to commemorate the victory over the Turks. However, over time, the croissant became associated with France and is now considered a staple of French cuisine.
